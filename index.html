My current role at the Center for Applied Structural Discovery and the Center for Personalized Diagnostics has allowed for me to utilize state of the art technology in various fields of research like bioenergy and medicine.

Areas of Research

Structural Biology

Projects include femtosecond time-resolved X-ray crystallography, Cryogenic electron microscopy, and X-ray emission spectroscopy. In the wetlab I have done everything from growing and harvesting cyanobacteria cell cultures to using high-performance computing to analyze cryo-EM data.

Read more

Bioinformatics & Biostatistics

First-hand experience analyzing various NGS and clinical data. I have applied a series of statistical tests on clinical data to find significant genes in breast cancer and developed several bash scripts to visualize the quality of reads and find variants in DNA and RNA samples.

Read more

Machine/Deep Learning

My current work with the Center for Personalized Diagnostics allows for me to utilize various standard and new machine learning techniques in order to better explore multi-omic datasets. We are using data-fusion in systems biology to explore for various neurogenetic diseases like dyslexia.

Software Development
